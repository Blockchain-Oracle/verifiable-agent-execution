## Pattern analysis

### What wins (across all 0G-sponsored events)

**1. One narrow primitive or mechanic — not a platform**
"Rugproof launches via TEE" wins over "a decentralized AI platform." "Agent memory transfer" wins over "multi-feature agent toolkit." The winning shape is always: one thing, done completely, with a demo that makes a judge understand it immediately.

**2. Visceral demo moment**
Every strong winner has a demo where something visibly _happens_ in 15 seconds:
- Battle AI: judges watch two AIs fight in real time
- AInfluencer: judges see an autonomous AI post a video with ZK proof on-chain
- Clampify: judges see a rugproof launch enforce itself on-chain
- OGxbt: judges see a verified trading signal with an on-chain proof link

The demo moment must exist and must be legible to a tired judge.

**3. Heavy use of 0G-specific components — not just "deploys a contract on 0G Chain"**
Winners use at least two of: Compute (inference), Storage (state/memory), iNFT, TEE/ZK. The more 0G-native primitives stacked on a single _coherent_ narrative, the stronger.

**4. Infra/SDK wedges beat consumer apps — especially in ETHGlobal-tier events**
Care-AI (agent SDK), OpenMemory (memory transfer), Clampify (rugproof primitive) all won because judges are ecosystem builders who want tools other devs will use. Consumer apps (chatbots, creative assistants) place lower or in smaller events.

**5. The Ajently shape (Mar 2026) is the most recent winner template**
"Build agents, deploy them, let others subscribe." Simple value chain. 0G Storage + Compute. Built in 4 days. Strong demo. This is now the prior-art baseline for any agent economy play.

### What does NOT win

- Generic "AI chatbot on 0G" — even when technically functional (0gChat placed $2K in a small Shanghai event; didn't win ETHGlobal-tier)
- Broad platforms without a single hero loop
- Submissions that only use 0G Chain (a smart contract) without integrating Compute, Storage, or TEE
- Concept decks or slide-only demos (explicitly disqualified by APAC rules)

## Recurrence note

0G Labs has run 7–8 hackathons/track-sponsorships in the last 12 months. This is above the soft-threshold (>4) for recurring-cycle risk. However:
- Prior winners are visibly external builders, not insiders
- The recurrence reflects genuine developer acquisition for a live mainnet (Aristotle) and product ecosystem (AIverse, Apollo AI Accelerator)
- No complaint threads found on X about rigged judging or insider winners

Verdict: recurring cadence is a marketing motion, not a scam-shape signal.

## APAC Hackathon vs ETHGlobal-tier: key differences

The APAC hackathon has a significantly higher submission bar than ETHGlobal track prizes:
- APAC requires: mainnet contract address, verifiable on-chain activity, 3-min demo video, README with architecture diagram, mandatory X post
- ETHGlobal track prizes at Cannes/Trifecta often accepted strong demos without all these requirements
- The APAC format rewards **depth and documentation** more than a quick hackathon demo

This means: the winner shape for APAC is closer to "startup-grade MVP" than "hackathon weekend build." 18 days remaining is viable for a narrow wedge with real 0G integration.
